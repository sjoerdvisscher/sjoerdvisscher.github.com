Welcome to w3future.com

 Sunday, April 29, 2001

I was wondering why my old posts were still on my home page. I had Radio Userland configured to show 14 days, but the homepage still had messages from April 5th. Apparently Radio Userland counts the number of days that actually have messages. I didn't expect that, but it's good to know that my weblog won't be empty if I don't have anything to say for a while.

The first Inside Macintosh was simple and low-tech. Easy to program for. But as systems evolve they get more complex. Then a new generation comes along, forgets everything we once knew, and all of a sudden things are simple again. It's the breathwork of technology. [Scripting News]
Hmm. I never thought of it that way. Simplicity can also come from experience and overview. If you look at XML, it is SGML with the experience of what can be left out. On the other hand, for people who don't know SGML, XML can look strange and complex. PI's, DOCTYPE, those clumsy DTD's with a weird looking syntax. XML might have been simpler if the designers would have started from scratch. SGML people complain that the W3C doesn't look enough at what they've already done, but perhaps they looked too much.
