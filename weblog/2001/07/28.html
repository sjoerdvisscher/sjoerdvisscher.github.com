I just received The Ultimate Hitchhiker's Guide by Douglas Adams, 5cm thick. It's 'required' reading for Q42, which I found out when I asked what the name "Q42" meant.

RDF Applications with Prolog (II)[Lambda the Ultimate]

I like the comparison with XSLT. I regard working with knowledge instead of structured data as the future of the world wide web, and Prolog works with knowledge. In this article you can get an idea how it works, although the tools aren't perfect yet: "I find it handy for debugging RDF documents". XML files shouldn't have to be debugged. Human readability is a real problem with RDF.

Brent Ashley released version 2.0 of JSRS, Javscript Remote Scripting. Once you've got such libraries, there's no excuse to keep making dull webpages. Brent also mailed me a word that applies to me currently: vicissitude. It's a fun idea to mail someone a word.
