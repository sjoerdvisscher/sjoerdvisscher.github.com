Website update: The buttons should now work in the Opera browser. They don't look as good as they do in IE and Mozilla, but at least they're there now, instead of some dots.

MSN Messenger Users Cite Spotty Service Throughout Thursday morning, people continued to report a string of seemingly unrelated glitches, including lost Hotmail address books and e-mail and missing MSN Calendar information. [Intense Beta Elite]

I hear from friends that some have lost the list of contacts they had in MSN Messenger, after the service was down for a whole day. But I haven't read much about it elsewhere. I use Trillian the connect with the MSN Messenger service, and it seems this saved me from loosing my contact list. And Microsoft is this company that is supposed to keep those Hailstorm services running?

Article writing is really hard. My last attempt is in hindsight a complete failure. The article is about the problems with XML and data modeling. It doesn't get the point across, if you even get a clue what the point is. A very useful conversation with Ron Pero showed me a different way to write the article. One problem I have is the use of the right words when talking about data models. Maybe it's because XML applies to all fields of the IT world. On the SML-DEV list we always talked about the map/list models and nodes, while to a programmer terms like associative arrays, dictionaries, structs and object make more sense. RDF use the terms properties, predicates and resources and database people compare XML with rows and columns. ...help me...

Sometimes nature can make things very clear. When do you know when you have to get out more often? When you find a pigeon on a nest with 2 eggs on your balcony. A severely frightened pigeon that is. That was last night. I'm glad she's back on her nest this morning. The balcony is a no-go zone for a while. I'm guessing the pigeon is enjoying it more than I would.
