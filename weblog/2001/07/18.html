 Wednesday, July 18, 2001

Joel on Software: Good Software Takes Ten Years. Get Used To It. Often when I start reading an article by Joel, I think: this can't be true, it doesn't apply to me, or that's somewhat overstated. This time it was: that doesn't apply to the internet. And everytime when I'm ready reading, I think: he's just plain right. Joel mentions Internet Explorer as an exception. I don't agree, Internet Explorer may allready seem feature complete, but that's just because it's users were not ready yet to push IE to it's limits. I want to be able to make custom HTTP requests, I want SVG, I want protected script code. IE really still has 5 years to go.

At the end, Joel suggests to make a ten year plan. But I can't imagine writing a 10 year plan for an internet product like Q42's Lime. It's not that I can't imagine to be working 10 years on the same product, but what features would a user expect 5 years from now? I have no idea.
