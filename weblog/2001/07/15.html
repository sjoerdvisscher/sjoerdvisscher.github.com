 Sunday, July 15, 2001

This morning I received an email from Dan Shappir. Very interesting and very helpful. One part drew my attention: "your technique shows how JavaScript supported delegates all along, so this is not some great C#/.NET invention." Reason enough to write a new article: Event Handlers and Callback Functions In Javascript.

Distributed Membership and Preferences. "Developers are now faced with a choice -- support Microsoft's membership system, and thereby feed customers to them, or develop an open, clonable and decentralized system, so that membership is a competitive space, not owned by one or two large companies." [Scripting News]

This is complicated stuff, and therefore very interesting. I like this: "Assume your data are not private". This is clear to a user. But to prevent spamming it should never be possible to harvest mail addresses. In fact, if someone knows my email address, he/she is allowed to know a lot more: name, age, gender, occupation (IMHO). I'm hoping that spammers will check this first and decide not to mail me, but that probably won't happen. Let's make the email address the key to publicly available data.
