No more files

John Udell about the future of filesystems: Unified Namespaces, Two-Way Linking, And Context Management. [owrede_log]

These developments, combined with the developments in file formats like XML, may ultimately evolve into the end of files and filesystems. In the future, each device will have a single database, where all information will be stored. I'm thinking of an RDF database, but if that means nothing to you, think of a Fontier database, or the Windows Registry. It's because it is limiting to have to divide the available information into blocks, i.e. files, and in this article you can read how filesystem designers struggle with that limitation.

One interesting fact about RDF databases is that each database is just a part of a virtual global database, and downloading files or any other kind of data transfer over a network will then all transform into a partial synchronisation of databases.
