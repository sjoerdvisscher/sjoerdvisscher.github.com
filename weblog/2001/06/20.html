Disenchanted: "Without discipline, a web log is a total waste of time. 'Blogs are typically confusing, directionless, and tiring to read. What they need is a role and a re-orientation." [Scripting News]

Undisciplined weblogs are not a waste of time. The claims made in this article are only valid if you see weblogs as journalism and try to read them from top to bottom. If you use an aggregator, most weblogs are fine the way they are now. These weblogs are not the paper your read when you come home from work, but rather the small talk you have with colleagues or friends during the day.

With syndication/aggregation of weblogs, it's useless to provide a storyline, or context, because the each blog item will end up randomly between items of other blogs. Even a meaningful comment is not always required. Links without comments are the equivalent of the "check this out" type e-mails that regularly get send around the office. Journalists may hate this kind of news spreading, but it works and is based on trusting the source that he/she will only point to items of interest. Often it becomes a contest who can find the coolest stuff on the web. Posting thoughtful comments with such links would be silly.

What is a requirement of a good weblog is to be original. It should either have original comments to other items on the web, or be an original filter on certain news sources so that result is more interesting than the combination of it's sources. A useful side-effect: original weblogs will automatically become more popular as they get linked to more often.
